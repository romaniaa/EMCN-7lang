nested">HELPING IMMIGRANTS REACH EMPLOYMENT (HIRE)

Immigrants who are ready, willing and able to obtain immediate employment in the Alberta labour market can do so with support from the Helping Immigrants Reach Employment (HIRE) program.

-bg">

nested">WHAT WE OFFER

Immigrant job seekers will benefit from:

    ">
  • Assistance with creating a realistic strengths-based career plan
  • Help with researching labour market and finding employment, education or volunteer opportunities
  • Support with creating targeted resumes and cover letters
  • Coaching on interview techniques and job search strategies
  • Help to understand hiring practices, Alberta employment customs and workplace responsibilities
  • Skill building through relevant workshops
  • Incidental workplace related supports that enable access to a specific job such as work boots or tools
-bg">

nested">HOW DOES THE PROGRAM WORK

Over a twelve-week period, clients participate in a series of sessions with a Career Practitioner and an Employer Liaison, with the aim of obtaining full-time employment. Between counselling visits, clients will have the opportunity to build their skills and learn about the labour market by attending workshops, job fairs, events and information sessions.

-bg">

nested">WHO CAN ACCESS THESE SERVICES?

Candidates must fulfill the following eligibility requirements:

    ">
  • Immigrant Albertans who are Canadian Citizens, Permanent Residents of Canada, or Refugees under the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act who have been given convention refugee status, and have been issued a ‘Notice of Decision’ and, have a valid Work Permit
  • Unemployed or marginally employed
  • 18 years of age or older
  • Ready, willing and able to work: the client’s employment goal is realistic and achievable in a twelve-week period, given the client’s abilities, experience, interests and the local job market
