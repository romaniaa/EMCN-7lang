nested">IMMIGRANT YOUTH EMPLOYMENT PROGRAM (IYEP)

The Immigrant Youth Employment Program (IYEP) assists youth to develop a broad range of employability and life skills in order to participate in the current and future labor market. By building responsibility through empowerment, youth obtain the tools to be self-sufficient and obtain meaningful employment.

-bg">

nested">WHAT WE OFFER

Graduates of IYEP benefit from:

    ">
  • Increased knowledge of workplace communication and Canadian workplace culture
  • Understanding of hiring practices, Alberta employment customs and workplace responsibilities
  • Essential skills and computer skills needed to obtain and maintain a job
  • Work experience in their area of interest, often leading to longer term employment
-bg">

nested">HOW DOES THE PROGRAM WORK

This program is delivered several times yearly to 16 eligible newcomer youth. The program is full-time, for 19 weeks in duration and includes:

    ">
  • Eight weeks in-class at the EMCN developing employability skills; a modest living allowance is provided
  • Eleven weeks in a full-time paid work experience placement in the participantâ€™s field of interest
  • Individual career counselling to formulate a career plan
  • Access to essential workplace supports and exposure courses, if required by an employer
-bg">

nested">WHO CAN ACCESS THESE SERVICES?

Candidates must fulfill the following eligibility requirements:

    ">
  • Between 15 and 30 years of age
  • Canadian citizen, permanent resident or a protected person as defined by the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act; Legally entitled to work in Canada and Alberta
  • Not currently in school
  • Not currently receiving Employment Insurance (EI)
  • Language proficiency of Canadian Language Benchmark 4 or higher in all areas: reading, writing, speaking and listening
  • High motivation and commitment to completing the program
