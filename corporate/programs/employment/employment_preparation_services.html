nested">EMPLOYMENT PREPARATION SERVICES

Our Employment Preparation Services equip newcomers of all educational backgrounds and levels of experience with employment-related skills and supports to access the Canadian labour market.

-bg">

nested">WHAT WE OFFER

Newcomers will benefit from:

    ">
  • Assistance with creating a realistic strengths-based career plan
  • Help with researching labour market and finding employment, education or volunteer opportunities
  • Support with creating targeted resumes and cover letters
  • Coaching on interview techniques and job search strategies
  • Help to understand hiring practices, Alberta employment customs and workplace responsibilities
  • Educational Planning
-bg">

nested">HOW DOES THE PROGRAM WORK

Through a series of sessions with an Employment Counsellor and an Employer Liaison, clients will make progress towards their personal career goal. Between counselling visits, clients will have the opportunity to build their skills and learn about the labour market by attending workshops, job fairs, events and information sessions.

-bg">

nested">WHO CAN ACCESS THESE SERVICES?

Newcomers are eligible to access Employment Preparation Services if they meet the following criteria:

    ">
  • Permanent resident, Convention Refugee, Live-in Caregiver or Protected Person as defined in Section 95 of the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act
  • “Willing to work” meaning that a client is willing to accept all types of opportunities that his or her abilities, skills, training, or experience enables him or her to do, and that he or she is willing to accept labour market conditions (for example, going rate of pay, hours of work)
