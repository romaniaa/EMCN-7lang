nested">IMMIGRANT YOUTH REPLANTING ROOTS

Immigrant Youth Replanting Roots: Planning Prosperous Futures is a collaborative project that assists young adults from the Karen, Syrian, and Iraqi communities to transition into the labour market or post-secondary education.

-bg">

nested">WHAT WE OFFER

Youth participating in the Replanting Roots program will benefit from:

    ">
  • Development leadership skills and build confidence
  • Information and supports on accessing employment opportunities and career opportunities
  • Support accessing resources
  • Information about Canadian norms, practices, and laws
  • Participate in recreational activities
-bg">

nested">HOW DOES THE PROGRAM WORK

Bi-weekly meetings are held by each ethno-cultural youth group separately, and a monthly forum brings all three groups of young adults together to discuss relevant topics to them. Programming is youth-led, meaning that the youth groups identify the issues and needs that they want to address.

-bg">

nested">WHO CAN ACCESS THESE SERVICES?


    ">
  • Young adults between 17 and 30 years old from the Karen, Iraqi or Syrian community who may face barriers trying to reach their goals
  • Participants should be members or willing to become a member of one of the following organizations:
    • Edmonton Karen Community Association
    • Iraqi-Canadian Association of Edmonton
    • Syrian Canadian Friendship Club
