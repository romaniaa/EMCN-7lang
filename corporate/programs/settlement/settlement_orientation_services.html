nested">SETTLEMENT ORIENTATION SERVICES

We provide newcomers with relevant, accurate, consistent, and timely information that is needed to make informed settlement decisions. We help newcomers understand life in Canada, including laws, rights, and the democratic system. With the right tools, individuals take charge of their own settlement and integration journeys and can effectively navigate Canadian systems.

-bg">

nested">WHAT WE OFFER

Newcomers benefit from:

    ">
  • Assistance with accessing health care, housing, income support, food resources and transportation
  • Services are offered in various languages by staff or we can provide interpretation
  • Assistance completing government forms and advocacy to access government programs and offices
  • Immigration related information and referrals
  • Commissioner of Oaths service
-bg">

nested">HOW DOES THE PROGRAM WORK

Through one or more sessions with a Settlement Practitioner, clients will make progress towards their personal settlement goals. Settlement practitioners work closely with other departments at EMCN to provide opportunities for clients based on their unique needs.

-bg">

nested">WHO CAN ACCESS THESE SERVICES?

Newcomers are eligible to access Settlement Services if they meet the following criteria:

    ">
  • Permanent resident
  • Convention Refugee
  • Live-in Caregiver, or
  • Protected Person as defined in Section 95 of the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act
